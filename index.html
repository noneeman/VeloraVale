Our story

The long way around to the same table

We found each other at a friend’s small dinner—two seats placed a little too close, a shared joke about the bread basket, and a walk afterward that turned into hours. What began as easy conversation became something quieter and deeper: two people who like the unglamorous parts of life as much as the beautiful ones.

Vale asked Velora on an ordinary Sunday, beside the garden they had planted together. There were no drones or flash mobs—only relief, laughter, and the sense that the answer had been forming for a long time. We are marrying in the Hudson Valley because it is where we breathe a little slower; we cannot wait to share that feeling with you.

The day

Saturday at Riverstone

A simple rhythm: arrive unhurried, celebrate fully, slip away when you are ready. If anything shifts, we will refresh this page and email the address on your invitation.

  1. 3:15–3:30 PM

    Guest arrival

    Please plan to be on property by 3:30 so everyone is seated before the processional. Valet at the main drive; follow signs to the ceremony lawn. If you need mobility assistance from parking, note it in your RSVP and we will arrange a golf cart.

  2. 4:30 PM

    Ceremony

    Outdoors, overlooking the river. In case of weather, we will gather in the estate’s glass conservatory—same time, same vows, a little more shelter from the sky.

  3. 5:15 PM

    Cocktail hour

    Terrace reception with passed bites, wine, and sparkling pours. The non-alcoholic list is written with the same care—ask a server for the evening’s pairings.

  4. 6:30 PM

    Reception · dinner & toasts

    Seated dinner in the barn hall, speeches kept blessedly short, then room for conversation before the band warms the floor. Dietary notes from your RSVP go straight to our caterer.

  5. 8:30 PM

    Dancing

    Comfortable shoes welcome. Last song near 11:00 PM, with coffee, tea, and small sweets by the fire for anyone who wants a quiet finish before heading home.

  6. Sunday, October 4 · 10:30 AM

    Optional farewell brunch

    No pressure—if you are still in town, join us for coffee and something warm at The Dutchess Inn’s private dining room. Add Sunday brunch to your RSVP notes so we can share an accurate head count with the inn.
